Business Continuity Planning

What is your company's risk tolerance? Can your company operate off-site after a major earthquake or fire? A company's ability to resume business operations immediately following a disruption can be the difference between success or failure. Critical tasks often require proprietary or complex systems and therefore might not be well-suited to conduct off-site. The determination of such critical business functions is the starting point. Companies–both large and small–frequently neglect business continuity planning due to perceived costs and efforts. What the same companies also neglect is how expensive it is not to plan. BGI can help you determine what is best for your business.

Our Services in Business Continuity Planning Include:

  • Redundancy
  • Off-Site Offices
  • Alternative Work Flow
  • Crisis Communications
  • Incident Command Chart Development and Structure
  • Business Interruption Insurance
  • Risk Mitigation Strategy

Security Strategy

Every organization, regardless of size, should have a comprehensive security strategy. A security strategy should consider many factors, among them the number of employees, budget, anticipated growth, business partnerships and subsidiaries, overseas presence, and key assets. Each company has unique business drivers, vulnerabilities, and priorities. BGI thoughtfully assesses those individual needs and creates a roadmap for a safe and secure future. For smaller companies, a sound security strategy can easily grow with the company. Larger companies with existing plans in place can utilize BGI in a value-based manner by identifying only those gaps requiring immediate attention. Don’t wait until a crisis or problem before acting.

Our Services in Security Strategy Include:

  • Enterprise Policies
  • Multi-Market Integrations
  • New Policy Creation
  • Assessment of Existing Policies and Procedures

Emergency Planning

Emergencies happen when you least expect them. They are never convenient or nicely timed. They can cause workflow disruption, conflict, future uncertainty, or worse. Strategic planning keeps your employees safe, your company's focus on the bottom line, and maintains investor confidence. Evacuation planning, crisis response protocol, floor warden programs, employee subsistence supplies, first aid / CPR training, and a well-crafted segment during the new employee on-boarding process are just some of the examples of strategies that your company should be embracing. BGI can help you determine where those vulnerabilities are and implement programs that are most needed. The method of integration and organizational cultural awareness are key considerations. Emergencies can be painful. Planning doesn't have to be.

Our Services in Emergency Planning Include:

  • Emergency Food, Water, and Medical Supplies
  • Earthquake Preparedness
  • Internal Staff Training
  • Tabletop Drills
  • Crisis Communication
  • Emergency Operations Centers – Design Models and Implementation

Security Design

Risks and threats come in all shapes and sizes. As a result, designing a security system for your facilities must encapsulate protection from vulnerabilities while balancing an aesthetic that captures the feel of your culture and environment. Pencils should be protected like pencils, and gold protected like gold. Architectural design and appropriate utilization of technology should be thoughtfully planned. Striking a balance between the necessary level of protection with cost and environmental feel are critical aspects of the perfectly designed system.

  • Strategic Planning
  • Adoption of Scalable Models
  • Technology Recommendations
  • Parking and Circulation
  • Target Hardening Methodology
  • Government Risk Profile Reporting

Real Estate Risk Assessment

The safety and security of physical structures is a part of a comprehensive risk strategy. Whether this occurs as a component of the due diligence process when vetting potential sites or occupancy, or whether it is property that your company is already contractually engaged with, a risk assessment is required to determine what risks and vulnerabilities exist and how they might be addressed through mitigation measures. Pre-occupancy assessments can leverage local and regional law enforcement in addition to architectural and engineering documents to determine the best course of action. Similar to any proactive philosophy, preventative retrofitting or tenant improvements in advance of a problem are a far better value and create long-term corporate best practices.

Our Services in Real Estate Risk Assessment Include:

  • Leveraging local law enforcement
  • Structural Design Due Diligence
