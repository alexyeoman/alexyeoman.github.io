Investigations

Investigations come in all types. They can be administrative, civil, or criminal. A properly managed investigation can reduce your company’s exposure to litigation, allow for injunctive relief in legal proceedings, and assist law enforcement authorities in enforcement and prosecution when needed. Case Study: Internal Theft of Computer Equipment

Client X is large company that operates a 24x7 office. They have contracted with a private security firm to provide uniformed guard services around the clock. They began to receive reports from their employees of missing items taken from their workspace on weekends and late at night. Most of the items were iPads and Apple laptops. After approximately six items were reported stolen in a four week span, BGI was asked to investigate. Since the company workspace is secure and access is restricted (employees are required to display their badge and swipe in through access readers at entry points), concerns of an insider (employee, contractor, vendor, etc.) committing such thefts began to increase. BGI's team of investigators mobilized and a confidential investigation ensued. Several strategies, some overt and some covert, were employed. Among them–additional guards, employee training and awareness, placement of cameras in the concentrated area of the thefts, and a comprehensive review of access logs and time correlative tracking of when confirmed thefts occurred. At the conclusion of the investigation, a suspect was identified (a member of the contract security staff) and was terminated. No reported thefts have occurred since.
