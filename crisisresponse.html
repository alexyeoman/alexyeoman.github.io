Case Study: Suspicious Package in the Mailroom

Client Y is a multi-national company with high profile leadership among it's founding members. In December during the holiday season, a wooden box was accepted to the mail room via Federal Express. The package had many suspicious characteristics. It was a wooden box nailed shut, it had an incomplete return address, it was apparently sent from a city in the Middle East, it was oddly weighted, and it was addressed to the company's CEO. Mail room staff, who had been previously trained by the US Postal Inspectors, immediately reported their observations to the security team. Security screened the package through an x-ray machine. Those findings raised additional concerns based on unusual characteristics visible inside. The local police department was contacted and the FBI's Bomb Squad was on-site within 40 minutes. Media became aware and was televising live from the scene. The surrounding buildings were evacuated quickly and efficiently. Work was disrupted for only three hours before the package was neutralized and nobody was hurt. It was not an explosive nor a bomb but rather an oddly shaped decorative vase, a gift for the CEO. Keeping your employees safe and mitigating work flow disruptions (three hours was an extremely fast resolution) is BGI's speciality. In this example, the keys to quick resolution were the pre-existing relationships with local and federal law enforcement, the proper training and awareness programs to empower the mail room staff, the invaluable x-ray machine as a tool of the investigation, and an internal culture that embraces security and safety.
