High-Risk Travel Security

The global economy has greatly changed the way companies do business today. Our borders are less clear, and a greater percentage of companies have some dependence on the worldwide economy, whether it's due to supply chains, overseas operations, customers in other countries, or simply producing products with global appeal. Your travelers are taking risks everyday that were previously rare or non-existent. Are they tracked? Are they trained? High risk and conflict zones around the world can often provide a backdrop for growth and opportunity for your company. Don't let safety and security concerns slow you down. They can be managed with a comprehensive travel security program. Ask your BGI representative to outline our philosophy and our approach.

Case Study: Protests in South America

Client Z is a global technology company who does extensive work in Central and South America. They have frequent travelers to approximately ten cities, many that are considered "moderate" or "high risk" by the US Department of State. In this example, a company executive made plans to travel to Santiago, Chile. Her hotel accommodations were booked at a globally known hotel chain. In this example, Hotel management was facing difficult labor negotiations with union groups. The disgruntled employees (topping out at nearly 180 at times) were protesting outside the hotel around the clock. Reports indicated that noise makers were being used and trash was strewn about the hotel property. Client Z, worried about their employee's safety and comfort, contacted BGI consultants for guidance. After a review of public sourced documents and confidential law enforcement intelligence via federal and local contacts, BGI provided a briefing of the protest, a written summary that could be easily shared with stakeholders, a suggestion of two nearby alternatives hotels, and a general safety advisory for overseas travel in the country. Due to BGI's quick response, within hours the travel plans were modified by the client and the executive stayed at another hotel. The adjustment caused minimal impact to her travel plans and no disruption to her business meetings.
