Workplace Violence Prevention

The media defines workplace violence as a violent gun-wielding ex-employee shooting his former colleagues because of being passed over for a promotion. Did you know these types of incidents–albeit terribly tragic and impactful–are statistically rare? The myth that people just snap or suddenly transition from a reliable, model employee to a homicidal shooter proliferates the headlines. In reality, 99% of workplace violence incidents do not actually involve the commission of violence but rather lead to harassment, workflow deficiencies, operational disruptions, wasted business opportunities, severed relationships, and unknown avoidable expenses. If you fail to plan, you can plan to fail. Proactive strategies are proven.

Our Services in Workplace Violence Prevention Include:

  • Crisis Team Formation
  • High Level Threat Assessments
  • Active Shooter Policy and Training
  • New Policy Creation
  • Manager & Supervisor Training
  • Interactive Enterprise Training Programs
  • Drills and Exercises

Law Enforcement Liaison

Risks don't sleep. When it comes to risk mitigation, leveraging government agencies for help is a core component of a global security strategy. From the local police department in your company's jurisdiction to federal agencies like the FBI, Secret Service, CIA, and the US Department of State. BGI works with a network of government agents in local cities around the United States and reaches out to many other continents for needed assistance and advice. Preventative measures and proactive strategies for risk mitigation create far better results and help reduce the need for crisis response.

Our Services in Law Enforcement Liaison Include:

  • Risk and Threats Assessments can often be provided to your organization –free of charge—by the Department of Homeland Security. Let BGI coordinate those efforts.

  • When does an internal or administrative investigation become a criminal investigation?
  • Training of Internal Security Teams about interfacing and communicating effectively with law enforcement officials.
